Gnocchi

Gnocchi are small dumplings that are traditionally made with potatoes, flour and eggs.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of russet potatoes
  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1 egg
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Steps

  1. Begin by boiling the potatoes in a pot of salted water until they are fork-tender. Drain the potatoes and let them cool for a few minutes.
  2. Peel the potatoes and pass them through a potato ricer or mash them with a fork.
  3. Spread the mashed potatoes out on a work surface and make a well in the center.
  4. Crack the egg into the well and add a pinch of salt and pepper.
  5. Slowly incorporate the flour into the mashed potatoes and egg, working the mixture with your hands until a soft dough forms.
  6. Divide the dough into 4 pieces and roll each piece into a long rope about 1/2 inch in diameter. Cut the rope into small pieces, about 1 inch long.
  7. To shape the gnocchi, roll each piece of dough against the back of a fork with your thumb, creating a small indentation on one side and ridges on the other.
  8. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Gently drop the gnocchi into the boiling water, being careful not to overcrowd the pot.
  9. Cook the gnocchi for about 2-3 minutes or until they float to the surface.
  10. Remove the gnocchi from the pot with a slotted spoon and toss with your desired sauce.
  11. Garnish with grated Parmesan cheese and chopped fresh parsley.

Note: You can also add some semolina flour or flour with a higher gluten content to improve the texture of the gnocchi, also you can add some ingredients to the dough like spinach or beetroot to give them a different color.
